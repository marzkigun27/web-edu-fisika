Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

Newton discovered that every mass attracts every other mass. This gravitational force is given by:

F = G (m₁ m₂) / r²

Tap the canvas to freeze/unfreeze the scene and see the force relation.

Kepler's First Law: Elliptical Orbits

Planets orbit in ellipses with the Sun at one focus. The ellipse is defined by its semi-major axis (a) and eccentricity (e).

Tap to freeze the motion and view current parameters.

Kepler's Second Law: Equal Areas

A line joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal time intervals.

Tap the red sector to freeze the sweep and display area.

Kepler's Third Law: Harmonies

T² ∝ a³: The square of the orbital period is proportional to the cube of its semi-major axis.

Tap to freeze two planets’ motion and view period ratio.

Example: Mercury's Orbital Period

For Mercury (distance 0.4 AU) versus Earth (1 year), Kepler’s Third Law gives:

Tₑ² / 1³ = Tₘ² / (0.4)³

  • (0.4)³ = 0.064
  • Tₘ² = 0.064, so Tₘ ≈ 0.253 years (≈92.3 days)

Tap to freeze Mercury’s orbit and overlay its period.
